### Solution : 
Here by writing ./ before the -- we made cat function ignore -- as a flag and treat it as a file instead. 
The backslash (\) is called an escape character. It tells the terminal, "Take the very next character literally, don't treat it as a special command."
In our case, the shell usually sees a space as a separator between two different arguments. By typing \ , we told the shell: 

"This space is actually part of the filename, not a gap between commands."

Hence the final command becomes : cat ./--spaces\ in\ this\ filename--
